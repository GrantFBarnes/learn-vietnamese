Vietnamese Alphabet

a, ă, â, b, c, d, đ, e, ê, g, h, i, k, l, m, n, o, ô, ơ, p, q, r, s, t, u, ư, v, x, y

Note the additional characters that are not in the English alphabet: ă,â,đ,ê,ô,ơ,ư

Note the missing characters that are in the English alphabet: f,j,w,z

The Five Accent Marks/Diacritics

The following marks are able to be applied to any vowels (including the ones above that already have marks).

Name Mark
acute ´
grave `
hook ̉
tilde ~
dot .

Here is an example of each mark used on "la" and the corresponding translation to show how these marks can make all the difference.

Example Translation
la to yell; scold
lá leaf (on a tree)
là to be
lả to faint
lã plain (plain water)
lạ strange

Here is how to pronounce each of these examples.

  • No Accent (la) - The level tone is produced at a relatively high pitch. Think of the pitch like when you stick out your tongue and say "ah" at the doctor's office.

  • Acute (lá) - This has a rising tone, so it has a very high and rising pitch when pronounced.

  • Grave (là) - This has a falling tone, so it has a rather low and falling pitch when pronounced.

  • Hook (lả) - This has a low-rising tone, so it starts at a low pitch and then rises when pronounced.

  • Tilde (lã) - This has a broken low-rising tone when pronounced. Meaning the voice starts low and rises abruptly, resulting in airflow being temporarily blocked causing a "break".

  • Dot (lạ) - This has a low tone, so it is pronounced using the lowest tone possible.
